Artist Portfolio — Aya Ito

Goal — Berlin-based painter Aya Ito needed a calm, image-led portfolio that could grow with new collections without ringing a developer each time. Approach — I mapped a single scroll anchored by hero, gallery grid, bio, and press snippets, then built the layout in plain HTML with Tailwind for spacing. Decap CMS powers editable sections for copy blocks, gallery ordering, exhibition dates, and contact links, while Netlify handles deploy previews and Plausible analytics. Outcome — The site ships under 1 MB, loads instantly on mobile, and streams meaningful metrics without invasive tracking. Aya now uploads new series, revises captions, and publishes press blurbs herself in minutes. Since launch, curators link directly to the gallery and enquiries land neatly in her inbox.

Tech note — Decap CMS · Netlify · Plausible

Local Studio — Parallel Hair

Goal — Parallel Hair, a two-chair studio in Leipzig, wanted a single page that listed services, hours, and neighbourhood info while taking €50 booking deposits online. Approach — I structured the page around clear blocks for pricing, stylists, testimonials, and a locality map, then wired a tailored contact form that posts to Supabase for logging and emails the team instantly. Stripe Payment Links handle the deposit flow, and the site runs as a static deploy with Netlify edge caching. Outcome — Clients now skim services in seconds, pay their deposit before arrival, and use the embedded map for directions. The team checks Supabase to review enquiries, exports data when needed, and stopped losing appointments to scattered DMs.

Tech note — Supabase form · Stripe Payment Link · Netlify

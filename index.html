About

I’m Micky Arratoon, a designer–developer in Leipzig. I build small, fast one-page sites for people who don’t want to wrangle Wix/WordPress or plugins. My approach is open-source where possible, with a simple editor so you can update text, images, and links after launch. Clear scope, tight timelines, calm communication—most projects ship in 48–72 hours once content is ready. If you want a custom page without the builder maze, I’ll take the wheel and get it live quickly.

Why Not Builders?

Builders are fine if you enjoy configuring tools. Most clients would rather skip the dashboards. I design and code the page, publish it fast, then hand over a calm /admin editor for simple edits.

  • No template maze, no vendor lock-in. Your repo lives in your account.
  • Performance and accessibility tuned by hand, not by plugins.
  • Open-source stack with documentation so any future dev can continue.

Artist Portfolio — Aya Ito

Goal. Berlin painter Aya Ito needed a quiet portfolio that foregrounds large works, keeps bio and press materials tidy, and lets her reorder pieces without emailing a developer.

Approach. I drafted a single narrative scroll—statement, gallery grid, exhibitions, contact—then wired Decap CMS collections for hero copy, artwork metadata, press links, and CV highlights. Netlify builds share preview links, while Plausible tracks views without cookies.

Outcome. The page ships under 1 MB, loads instantly on mobile, and every text or gallery change publishes via a clean /admin editor. Curators now receive a consistent link, and Aya uploads new series minutes after shoots.

Tech note — Decap CMS · Netlify · Plausible

Local Studio — Parallel Hair

Goal. Parallel Hair wanted to reduce Instagram DMs by pointing clients to a single page with services, hours, map directions, and a €50 booking deposit.

Approach. I mapped sections for hero, stylists, pricing, testimonials, and locality info, then wired a Netlify form that posts to Supabase for logging while emailing the team. Stripe Payment Links handle deposits, and the site deploys statically for speed.

Outcome. Clients skim pricing, pay deposits before visiting, and use the embedded map for directions. The team exports enquiry logs from Supabase, and bookings arrive with fewer back-and-forth messages.

Tech note — Supabase form · Stripe Payment Link · Netlify

Statement

I chase the slippages that happen when printed matter leaves the archive and returns to the street. My process begins with municipal posters, club handouts, and bus shelter ads that I scan and reprint using risograph, silkscreen, and relief stamping. Layers misregister on purpose; seams stay visible so you can read how the image was built. I pair these prints with lightboxes, salvaged metal, and projector glare to create installations that feel half-finished, as if they might keep editing themselves. The work asks how unofficial histories survive in public space and who keeps them legible.
